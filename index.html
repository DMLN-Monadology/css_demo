CSS & Rossiya

With over 1/8th of the world's land mass at 17 million square kilometers, Russia spans across 9 time zones and is the largest country on Earth.

Originally inspired by Japanese Daruma dolls, the Russian nesting Matryoshka dolls attained international fame by winning the bronze medal at the 1900 World Fair in Paris. It is now one of the most popular souvenir in the world today.

Alexander Nevsky (1221-1263 C.E.) Repelling several invasions, Alexander resided as the Grand Prince of Kiev during some of the most difficult times in Kievan Rus' history. Modern generations are reminded of his legacy by the critically acclaimed film by Sergei Eisenstein in 1938.

Peter I (1672-1725 C.E.) Peter the Great led Russia to victory in the Great Northern War, securing its place as the new dominant power in Northern Europe for centuries. His legacies include the founding of St. Petersburg, the jewel of European Russia, as well as Enlightenment reforms that modernised Russia.

Yuri Gagarin (1934-1968 C.E.) On April 12th 1961, Gagarin became the first human to journey into outer space as the pilot of a Soviet Vostok spacecraft. For this feat, he was awarded the title Hero of the Soviet Union. Yuri's Night on April 12th, the anniversary of his 108 minutes space flight, is still celebrated worldwide to this day.
